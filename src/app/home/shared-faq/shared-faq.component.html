Each Interfriends savings cycle lasts for six months. All members start and end their cycles at the same time.

There are two cycles in a year. Jan to June and July to Dec.

Interfriends has a policy of deciding who can collect their payout first, second, third, or sixth month.

Before the cycle starts, all members propose when they want to collect their bulk payout.

Interfriends reviews this in line with our policy and accepts or declines by offering an alternative bulk payout month.

New members in the circle will always collect their bulk payout last in the cycle.

As members stay with Interfriends for a longer period, they build trust and credibility while also growing their Provident with the group. All these play a role in deciding when a member can collect their payout within the six month cycle.

Interfriends is completely free to use. There are no monthly or annual fees to pay.

There are no charges to the members; however, every member contributes 10% of their total bulk payout into a Provident pot. This Provident is held for the member as a risk pot.

The total Provident amount accrued by a member is returned to them should they decide to leave the group at some point.

Interfriends Co-operative is registered in England as a registered society under the Co-operative and Community Benefit Societies Act 2014.
