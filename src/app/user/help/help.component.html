Who are we?

Interfriends is a savings club that helps members to diligently save money and also manage their finances by taking part in a savings circle.

Interfriends is also a Co-operative where members become shareholders in the Interfriends Co-operative business.

I can save my money with the bank, why should I join Interfriends?

By joining a savings circle, you willingly hold yourself to account by agreeing to diligently save a specific amount each month with other circle members.

By joining Interfriends savings circle, you cultivate the most important habit of diligent saving, even when you donâ€™t feel like it.

By joining Interfriends, you can have the flexibility of receiving an early bulk payout without having to wait till the end of the cycle.

With traditional banks, you can only withdraw what you have saved and nothing more.

How is Interfriends managed?

Interfriends is managed by a core team, the Operations Team. The team is responsible for the day-to-day running of the group.

In addition, there is also the Interfriends Board, which makes strategic decisions that govern how Interfriends is run.

Interfriends has been in operation for 15 years. We are trusted by our members for professionalism, openness, and transparency.

We are a Co-operative, which means we also do many things together, and our members share in the values of the Co-operative.

Interfriends membership is by recommendation only, which means members only recommend friends and family they can trust.

I want to join Interfriends but I still have questions

Yes, we fully understand your concerns about joining us for the first time. Like any new venture, it is okay to have doubts even after speaking with us.

If you are still in doubt about the group, speak to your recommender or feel free to request to speak to other members of the group to seek their views about how they have found it before making up your mind. If you still have concerns, you can, by all means, we suggest you speak to Sam Buah, the chair of the board who will try to assist and answer any concerns you may have.

What happens once I am accepted as a member?

Once you are accepted as a member, you will receive an email notification that your membership has been approved

Your welcome email will contain a link. Follow this link to complete your membership registration.

Once your membership is completed, you will be included in the circle your recommenders belong to

When do I start paying my monthly contributions (savings)

There are two cycles of 6 months each. November to April and May to October. As a group, we all start and end the cycle at the same time. Depending on when you join, you will either start your savings in November or May.

Is there a minimum or maximum amount to save?

The minimum saving is Â£50 however, there is no maximum. We strongly recommend that you start with an amount that you are comfortable with. You will always be given the opportunity to increase, decrease or suspend your membership before every new cycle starts.

Do you need a specific number of members to save the same amount as me before I can start?

No, with Interfriends, you join a circle with members who save different amounts. What you save is specific to you and does not depend on any other member within the circle, although you all form part of the circle

Do I always have to wait till the end of the cycle to receive my bulk payments?

No, you may opt to receive your bulk funds after the first, second, third, up to the sixth month. .

The month a member receives their bulk payout is dependent on the amount of Provident they have accrued in the group.

This means a new member with no provident will automatically receive their bulk funds/payout at the end of the cycle.

Will the amount of bulk funds I receive be affected if another member of the group delays or misses their monthly payment?

No, as a group, we have tried to minimise most of the potential risk exposure to the group

The Provident fund helps us to step in if a member is not able to pay or misses their payment

Every time you are due to receive your bulk funds, we will deduct 10% and keep it for you as a safety net.

The provident is recorded and saved for you. Over time, your provident fund grows and becomes sufficient to cover you if you are unable to make your expected monthly savings

What if I decide to collect my bulk funds last all the time, will you still deduct provident?

The provident deduction is mandatory for all members regardless of when they choose to collect their bulk funds.

The principle of the provident is also to allow the group the ability to leverage our combined finances for the benefit of the wider group.

Interfriends is a cooperative, so we do things with a slightly different approach

How often can I receive my bulk funds in a cycle?

Each member is entitled to their bulk funds payments once within the six months (cycle). This amount is equal to six times your monthly contribution less the agreed provident fund deduction.

This means if you save Â£100 a month, you will be paid Â£540 as a lump sum and Â£60 kept as your Provident.

How long will you keep my provident and where will you keep it?

So long as you remain a member of Interfriends, we will hold the provident fund for you in Interfriendsâ€™ dedicated bank account

Whenever a member decides to leave the group, all their accrued provident funds will be returned to them

What do you do with the provident funds apart from using it as a safety net and to determine the size of the assistance I get if I need one?

Apart from using your provident as your safety net, you can also see it as another form of savings you are gradually accruing or a rainy day.

As a group, we also engage in specific profit-generating ventures with a portion of the provident.This means while your provident is kept with us, you will also be generating some profits from it.

The board may consult you prior to engaging in new investment ventures. There are many things we can collectively achieve together as a group than as individuals.

So it is possible that I will lose my provident?

As much as possible we hold the bulk of your provident in our dedicated bank account. It is only a percentage we may invest to generate profit for you. So it is very unlikely that you will ever lose all your provident plus members have the right to decide not to be involved in specific investment opportunities

Can I decide to leave Interfriends anytime I want?

Yes, you can leave Interfriends anytime by serving us with the correct minimum notice period.

I can see there is a lot of work with Interfriends, do you charge any membership fees?

No, there is no charge to join Interfriends, neither do we charge ongoing membership fees. Interfriends is not a profit-making business venture. Our aim is to help ourselves to achieve things we may not be able to achieve individually.

Is there a specific day I have to make my monthly payment?

As a group, we recognise that members receive their salaries at different times in the month so we are also flexible with when you are expected to make your monthly payment. You are allowed to pay anytime between the 1st and the last day of each month.

What happens if I miss or make payments late.

The group relies so much on the trustworthiness of the members however, we also appreciate that we have to do more to ensure we are all responsible and accountable for the good of the group. For this reason, we also operate our own credit scoring scheme. We record missed or late payments and use this to make future decisions.

Can I change my savings amount for future cycles?

Yes, at the end of the six-month cycle, members can upgrade, downgrade, keep to their existing savings, or leave the group altogether if they want to

Should a member decide to leave the group, all their accrued Provident funds will be returned to them, less any money they owe the group at the time of leaving.

Will Intefriends help me if I require additional financial help?

Interfriends operate a small assistance scheme aimed at helping members to raise extra money over and above their bulk funds. Extra assistance can be requested anytime during the cycle.

If I need extra financial help, is there a maximum amount I can receive?

Yes, we do not have unlimited amounts to help every member so there is a cap on the maximum we can offer. This maximum amount is also dependent on the amount of provident funds the member has accrued. We will also take into consideration your repayment habit and history (this is the reason why as a group we operate our own credit scoring system to help us make fair decisions). The maximum amount given is subject to the availability of funds and other factors.

How long do I have to repay if you give me a loan?

To minimise the risk exposure to the group, we require that all assistance offered to members are repaid over 12 months. Under exceptional circumstances, additional time may be allowed to repay the loan, this will be reviewed on a case by case basis.

Is Interfriends all about savings?

No, Interfriends is not only about savings; there are a lot more we can succeed together as a Co-operative.

We also operate a cooperative business, which is fully owned by our members.

How do you currently operate and manage your risks?

a. As a group, we are trying to minimise any risk of financial loss to any member by instituting various strategies to remove or minimise potential risks. For example, all new members of the group collect their bulk funds last and work their way up in subsequent cycles until they have accrued sufficient provident funds to also allow them to collect their bulk funds early.

b. Before each cycle starts we give the opportunity to members to reconsider their savings; either to increase, decrease, suspend or leave the group. This flexibility allows members to save relevant amounts applicable to their individual circumstances.

c. Once a member decides to leave the group, they can only be readmitted to the group after a minimum of 12 months and this will be subject to the approval of the board.

d. All members are subject to a percentage deduction from their bulk funds within each cycle. This deduction is set at 10% of total savings per cycle.

This is not a charge but a safety fund saved on behalf of the member. This will be used to cover the member in the event of an emergency

e. In the event a member defaults and the money cannot be immediately recovered, their accrued provident will be used to cover/offset any money they owe.

f. The board reserves the right to ask a member to resign from the group should they find or have a reason to believe that such a member poses a substantial risk to the group.

g. Any member can recommend a friend and or relation to join the group before the beginning of each cycle. The board will ultimately decide if this person should be admitted to the group or not.

h. Members have access to their dashboard which has every detail of their savings, provident, loans, profits etc.

i. A memberâ€™s dashboard can be accessed by entering the unique ID provided to the member during registration.

j. At the end of each cycle, members will be issued with their most up-to-date financial statements in the group.

k. If the group decides to invest any capital you hold with the group, you will be notified and given the option to opt-in or out should you wish.

l. The board will make decisions on behalf of the members and in the interest of the group.

m. From time to time members will be allowed to sit in board meetings as an observer(s). The board will inform members and request for expression of interest where this is necessary.

n. As a minimum, the board will hold a general meeting for members once a year. The date and time will be communicated to members.

o. In the interest of the group, board members are expected to act honestly, professionally and in the fear of God to represent the interest of the group.

p. Board members should act in the absolute interest of the group and not in a way that will result in any financial loss to the group.

q. Interfriends is a voluntary group and members will be called upon from time to time support with their time and resource

r. Members may be allowed to become board members on a rolling basis and for a set period to be decided.

s. Interfriends is governed by a board that run and makes key decisions on behalf of the group.

t. In the interest of the whole membership, Interfriends will be governed by policies agreed by the board and which sets out the minimum expected behaviour for each of the policy areas. Where appropriate, policies will be shared with the members of the group.

Is Interfriends legal

Interfriends Co-operative is registered in England as a registered society under the Co-operative and Community Benefit Societies Act 2014.

How do you get the people to join in the circle

Interfriends Circle operates by recommendation only.

To join a circle, two existing members from that circle should recommend that you join.

Each circle also has a circle lead who will approve new members to join.

Circle membership is currently limited to up to 25 members. All recommended by existing members of the circle. .

Do I have to save a specific amount each month?

Yes, you choose a specific amount to save each month and stick with it throughout the cycl.

How long is each savings cycle?

Each Interfriends savings cycle lasts for six months. All members start and end their cycles at the same time.

There are two cycles in a year. Jan to June and July to Dec.

When do I collect my payout for the cycle?

Interfriends has a policy of deciding who can collect their payout first, second, third, or sixth month.

Before the cycle starts, all members propose when they want to collect their bulk payout.

Interfriends reviews this in line with our policy and accepts or declines by offering an alternative bulk payout month.

New members in the circle will always collect their bulk payout last in the cycle

As members stay with Interfriends for a longer period, they build trust and credibility while also growing their Provident with the group. All these play a role in deciding when a member can collect their payout within the six month cycle

Are there any membership fees to pay?

Interfriends is completely free to use. There are no monthly or annual fees to pay.

Are there any other charges

There are no charges to the members; however, every member contributes 10% of their total bulk payout into a Provident pot. This Provident is held for the member as a risk pot.

The total Provident amount accrued by a member is returned to them should they decide to leave the group at some point.

Why do you have to deduct the 10% provident

All members are subject to a percentage deduction from their bulk payout.

This deduction is set at 10% of total savings per cycle. This is not a charge but a safety fund kept on behalf of the member

This will be used to cover the member in the event of an emergency when they are not able to make their usual monthly savings.

How do I make my monthly payments?

All payments are made via online banking into our dedicated bank account. You will be given our bank account details to make your regular payments using your unique ID, which will be provided to you .

We do not accept any cash payments or any other form of payment

What is Interfriends Co-operative

The Interfriends Co-operative is a registered business arm of Interfriends where members can invest as shareholders.

The Co-operative business uses the investment to run the business for the benefit of the members.

How do I build my trust with Interfriends?

While we depend on our members to act in a trustworthy way, we also make use of technology to assign a Trust Score to each member

As members continue making regular payments on time, they build their Trust Score. This is used to make decisions about the members

How do you manage your risks?

As a group, we minimise risks of financial loss to members by instituting various risk mitigating measures, including the Provident fund deduction, assigning Trust Scores to members, and above all, following a strict process of approving new members
